<p><p>Wireless locking systems encompass various technologies, including Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, RFID, and others. Bluetooth locks provide short-range access, ideal for personal devices, ensuring user convenience. Wi-Fi locks connect through existing networks, enabling remote access and management from smartphones or computers. RFID systems use radio waves for keycard or fob-based entry, typically found in commercial settings. Other types may include Z-Wave and Zigbee, which facilitate connectivity in smart home ecosystems. Each technology offers distinct benefits for security and access control across different applications.</p></p>
