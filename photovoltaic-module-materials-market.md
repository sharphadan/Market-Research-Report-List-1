<p><p>The photovoltaic module materials market primarily consists of monocrystalline and polysilicon types. Monocrystalline silicon is known for its high efficiency and durability, constructed from single-crystal structures, making it ideal for space-constrained installations. In contrast, polysilicon, made from multiple silicon crystals, offers a more cost-effective option with slightly lower efficiency. Both materials contribute to the growing demand for solar energy solutions, with monocrystalline often favored for residential and premium applications, while polysilicon dominates the utility-scale market due to lower production costs.</p></p>
