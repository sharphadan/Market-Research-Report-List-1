<p><p>The Cervical Total Disc Replacement Device market features two primary types: Metal On A Biocompatible Material (M-O-B) and Metal On Metal (M-O-M). M-O-B devices combine metal components with materials designed to minimize adverse biological responses, promoting better integration with surrounding tissues. Conversely, M-O-M devices utilize two metal surfaces for articulation, offering durability but potentially raising concerns about wear particles and long-term biocompatibility. Both types aim to alleviate pain and restore motion in patients with cervical disc degeneration.</p></p>
