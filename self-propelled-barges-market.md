<p><p>The self-propelled barges market encompasses two primary types: inland and ocean barges. Inland barges are designed for navigating rivers, canals, and lakes, facilitating the efficient transportation of goods over short to medium distances, often connecting to land-based transport systems. In contrast, ocean barges are built for maritime navigation, enabling the movement of larger cargo volumes across open waters. Both types play crucial roles in the logistics industry, offering specialized designs suited to their respective environments and operational needs.</p></p>
